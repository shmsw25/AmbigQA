This demo is based on DPR, consisting of a dense retrieval model and a span extraction model.
First, a dense retrieval model retrieves the top K passages from Wikipedia by finding passages using maximum inner product search between question representations and passage representations (K=100).
Then, a span extraction model reads the question and passages, and outputs passage selection scores (P(p|q)) and span answers from each passage (P(a|p,q)).
The final answer is determined based on p(a,p|q)=P(p|q)P(a|p,q).

The full DPR model is resource expensive, requiring over 150GB of RAM. In order to fit in 20GB of RAM, we massively pruned the Wikipedia corpus, from 21M passages to 1.7 passages.
This variant achieves 36EM on NQ open (the full DPR model achieves 41EM).
